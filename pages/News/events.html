Artes Ita Paskua Admist COVID-19

This year’s Artes Ita Paskua is now celebrated physically which started through the lighting of Christmas Tree in front of Vigan City Hall

November 22, 2021 | The annual Artes Ita Paskua of the City Government of Vigan got underway last December 07 – 17 of 2020 with the new normal set-up in which most of the events were done through online and the others were with limited participants to comply into the Minimum Health Standards set by the City Covid-19 Task Force. Despite all the odds brought on by the pandemic, the Christmas vibes in the Heritage City continued.

To prevent mass gathering while still enjoying the season, the City Events, Promotions and Protocol Office steered barangay and home based competitions such as the Inter-Barangay Christmas Installation Contest which was held last December 7-8, 2020 wherein different barangays of the city putted up their Nativity Scene, Christmas Trees and other colorful and bright Christmas related decorations in their respective Barangay Halls and covered courts made into recycled materials.

The judging of entries as well as the awarding of winners were done by visiting them personally in their barangays, in the end the following were conclude as winners to received cash prizes and trophies through CEPPO Edred Piamonte, City Treasurer Milagros Contreras and City Assistant Treasurer Edwin Remigios.

Heritage City Vigan Observes New Normal Solidarity Week

The heritage city of Vigan celebrated its second year of new normal solidarity week where most of the activities are held virtually

September 8, 2021 | The City Government of Vigan, under the direction of Mayor Juan Carlo Medina, organized a number of events last September 6–16, 2021, as the Heritage City of Vigan celebrated its second New Normal Solidarity Week. The celebration was formally kicked off by the annual On-the-Spot Painting and Letras Y Figuras competition at Fil-Spanish Friendship Park, Mira Hills, Vigan City.

Due to the COVID-19 danger, the majority of the events were conducted remotely, with entrants sending their entries via recorded files. Mayor Medina stated that the celebration's main goal is to preserve Biguenos culture and heritage in the face of today's difficult and dynamic environment. He continued, "May our dedication to heritage conservation continue for the continued prosperity of our beloved heritage city for this is our distinct identity to the world."

Pride Virtual Grand Fiesta in Vigan City

The heritage city of Vigan celebrated its second year of new normal solidarity week where most of the activities are held virtually

June 14, 2021 | The City Government of Vigan, in collaboration with Maris Ti Ayat Inc., held the first-ever Pride Virtual Grand Fiesta last June 14–30, 2021, in spite of the challenges brought on by the pandemic. The event featured a variety of activities to mark the city's Pride month and to further the support provided to the aforementioned community.

The month-long celebration got underway on June 14 when Maris Ti Ayat Inc. officers and members under the direction of acting president Jay Leonard Roldan gave out Abel facemasks and Pride pins to city employees.

This is being done, according to Maris Ti Ayat Inc., "to encourage the observance of health protocols while simultaneously promoting our own Abel products and the tourism industry of the city as a whole because they are one of the most affected industries in this trying time." said Roldan.
