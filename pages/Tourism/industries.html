Industries

Burnay

One of the two ceramic styles used in the Ilocos region—Burnay—is made there, particularly in the City of Vigan, where Chinese immigrants brought the technique sometime in the late 18th or early 19th century.

Damili

The molding-related llocano word. One of Vigan's traditional industries is the production of terracotta or red clay. It has been practiced since the 19th century and is currently carried out in some Vigan barangay homes. Visitors from both domestic and foreign countries can stop by the barangays of Ayusan Sur, Barraca, Bulala, and Salindeg, Paratong, Paoa, and Pong-ol. within these barangays. Bulala is most known for producing damili, therefore most frequented.

Abel Loom Weaving

The abel is a traditional Vigan and Ilocos-area woven product. The abel fabric is known for being areas of strength for a, material. The texture is areas of strength for so lovely that a few families have them as treasures that keep going as long as their classical decorations.

Vigan empanada

The name "Empanada" comes from a Spanish word that means "to wrap in bread." It is the most common street food in Vigan Ilocos Norte and one of the best meriendas there.
