Tourist Attractions

Calle Crisologo

The most photographed street in Vigan, Calle Crisologo this known for its cobblestone pavements and old Spanish structures. These structures used to be houses of wealthy families during the Spanish colonial period but several were destroyed by fire during the Japanese occupation of the Philippines. The burned houses were later on reconstructed and have been converted into inns, cafes, and shops selling items such as accessories, furniture, antiques, and other souvenir items.

Baluarte

The residence of Chavit Singson, a former governor. He opened it to the public to share his love of animals and the natural world. It has a butterfly garden, a miniature zoo, and an animal encounter show. Food hovels and keepsake shops are accessible in the region.

Plaza Salcedo

This is a public park in Vigan, Ilocos Sur, Philippines. The park is the longer arm of an L-shaped open space where a popular fountain display is located.

Mira Hills
Fil-Spanish Friendship Park

For both locals and visitors to Vigan, Mira Hills Fil-Spanish Friendship Park serves as an educational and recreational amenity. The pool and amphitheater are among the park's features. Vigan Cathedral

The Metropolitan Cathedral of the Conversion of St. Paul the Apostle, or Vigan Cathedral, is a Catholic cathedral in Vigan, Ilocos Sur, Philippines. It houses the cathedral of the Archdiocese of Nueva Segovia. One of the most remarkable historic sites in Vigan, it was included in the Historic Town of Vigan's 1999 UNESCO World Heritage Site declaration. It is a historic church in the Ilocos area, located next to Burgos Plaza and in front of Salcedo Plaza.
