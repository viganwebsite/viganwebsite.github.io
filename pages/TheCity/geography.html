Vigan's Topography

The majority of Vigan's land area is flat, but Brgy. V, Tamag, and Paoa do have 20-degree slopes. 36.60% of the 2,886 hectares of land are used for residential purposes, while 45.18% are used for agriculture.

Deeply cut valleys make up the drainage network along the eastern edge of the Vigan-Bantay plain. These valleys' patterns are highly influenced by major faulting trends, especially those that run north and east. On the north and south, the Mestizo River, whose water release originates from Bantaoay head water, and the Vigan River, respectively, drain Vigan.

The slope of the Vigan-Bantay plain ranges from 0% to 8%, and the relief is actively level to undulating. Particularly Vigan is largely level with a few hills in the Brgy. The elevation of Tamag, Bulala, and Salindeg, which can occasionally exceed about 50 meters, is between 3% and 8%.

The eastern edge of the plain has a slope that ranges from high to very steep (40–60%), and the relief frequently rises to an elevation of more than 600 meters, notably in the northern and eastern parts.

The Vigan Gap Hill, where the continuously shifting Vigan River cuts its course, is the main topographic feature in the eastern part of the town. In the east of Vigan, around 10 kilometers, is where you'll find The Gap Hill.

Surface Drainage
The Mestizo River

Vigan was once totally encircled by water that jutted out from the shore like a triangle island, according to a chart from the sixteenth century. Geological observation supported this finding because it could be determined that the water bodies in the vicinity of Vigan were the primary channels that were then running in a westerly direction and branching as the river sliced the Vigan ridge.

The Climate
in Vigan City

Vigan has a climate that is classified as having the first climate type in the Philippines, which has two distinct seasons. The dry season often starts in November and lasts through April, peaking in the hot months of April and May. June through October are the wettest months, with August being the wettest month. The greatest recorded rainfall is 693.3 mm, and the average annual rainfall is 190.683 mm.

26 °C is the average temperature. The average high and low temperatures are 30.9 °C and 21.1 °C, respectively. Every year, 7–10 typhoons pass through Vigan. The relative humidity is 81% on average.
